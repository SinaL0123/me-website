What is the story of your name

My name is Sina and my Chinese name is Shuning. My parent gave me the name because they want me to be a quiet girl who likes reading books. And I gave myself the English name based on my Chinese name's pronunciation.

what are two of your hobbies. describe what you like about them.

Photography

One of my biggest hobbies is photography. I first started with a digital camera because it was easy to use and came with preset filters—perfect for someone like me who knew nothing about Photoshop. I really like Fujifilm, and my very first camera was a Fujifilm XT-30. I used to carry it almost everywhere when I traveled, and it always gave me satisfying results. Earlier this year, inspired by a friend, I switched to film photography. Surprisingly, I fell in love with it—the stunning colors and warm tones are something digital just can’t fully capture. I even dropped my cognition class this semester so I could take a film photography course instead, because I wanted hands-on experience in developing my own rolls. Overall, photography gives me a strong sense of fulfillment and accomplishment, especially when I manage to capture a fleeting moment of beauty through my viewfinder.


Horror Movies

My second hobby is watching horror movies. I especially enjoy cult classics and religion-themed horror films. Whenever I feel bored, I often search online for high-rated horror movies and then watch them alone under the blanket. Whether it’s well-known titles like The Shining, The Conjuring, It, and Smile, or more niche ones like Terrified and Incident in a Ghostland, I enjoy them all. What fascinates me most is the immersive atmosphere that horror films create—it makes me feel like I’m inside the story. I also love the rhythm of jump scares and the element of surprise. For me, the most exciting part is that even when I’m mentally prepared, I still end up getting scared, and that’s exactly what makes horror movies so powerful and fun to watch
